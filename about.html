How I got started

A few years ago, I was in my neighborhood grocery store. I was buying a sourdough baguette for my family’s dinner. I noticed that the loaf was about four or five inches smaller in length, and the price of the loaf was in the past and had gone up 20% in price. I felt like I was being taken advantage of by the grocery store. I went home and started to study and research how to make sourdough bread; that was the beginning. In December 2019, my wife asked me if I would bake some bread for her friends. I baked two loaves, and she was back the next day asking if I would mind baking some more, and from there, things have just grown. For each of the last three years, I have baked over 100 loaves as Christmas gifts for our friends and neighbors. My wife delivered freshly baked, warm sourdough bread to our happy and excited friends. She often would return home to tell me that people love the bread and those compliments were flattering. For over three years, my wife has asked me if I would please consider selling my bread. I have been very reluctant to do so. I have already gone through turning other hobbies that I have or had into businesses, and it is not long before I hate that hobby. It is different this time. I only bake bread once or twice a week and limit the number of loaves to only 8. I deliver the bread within two blocks of my home, or the customer can pickup the bread at my home. And that is where the name of my little bakery comes from, “Kirby2UBread”.

Why My bread is Unique

I experimented with different kinds of sourdough starters when I first started making sourdough bread. Some starters had very little tang of the sourdough, and others were so sour that they made my mouth pucker. I am using a starter that is over 35 years old, and for me it is the perfect blend of sour. Recently, my wife and I went to dinner at a very well-known restaurant for its sourdough bread. I eagerly started eating their bread and heard my wife say, “Why am I eating this bread? Yours is so much better.” I had to agree. I believe I found the right techniques, from no kneading to spending hours of blending and kneading. I am constantly experimenting. I believe I have developed the right portions of sourdough starter to flour, water, letting the dough rise, proofing, and getting the sourdough starter at its apex of growth to provide that balance of taste. It definitely is not a science for me; it is a touch and feel that only comes from making hundreds of loaves.
